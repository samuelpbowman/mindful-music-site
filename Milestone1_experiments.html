Experiments for Requirement Gathering Phase¶

In this notebook, I will perform a number of experiments on the data gathered from those who took our survey. We asked 18 questions dealing with demographics, sleep health, and their relationship with music in order to gauge how well our platform would be received.

Limitations¶

Most of our participants are college students, so the data will largely be restricted to a younger demographic. While this may limit our reach at first, I believe that younger people (especially those in college) are generally more willing to try new things, so there should not be much of an issue with the data being skewed in this manner. Also, while our sample size isn't massive, it is large enough to get a feel for where the general population which we interviewed stand as a whole.

I also noticed a few people did not answer each question. I believe that we should have marked certain questions as must be answered because having entries who only partially participated may also undermine the validity of our data. As a counter-measure, I shall remove non-compliant respondents from studies which they may skew on a case-by-case basis.

In [152]:
for index, row in second_ex.iterrows():
    # for some reason, the sheet we got had the major column 
    # set to NaN if there was no major selected
    if type(row[major_question]) is not str:
        second_ex.drop(index, inplace=True)
    else:
        majors = [s.strip().lower() for s in re.split('/|and|;|&', row[major_question])]
        # more than one major necessitates the inputs being broken up
        if len(majors) > 1:
            second_ex.drop(index, inplace=True)
            offset = second_ex.size / 3
            for i in range(0, len(majors)):
                second_ex.loc[offset + i] = [row[occupation_question], majors[i], row[interest_question]]
        elif 'Csci' in majors:
            second_ex.drop(index, inplace=True)
            second_ex.loc[second_ex.size / 3] = [row[occupation_question], 'computer science', row[interest_question]]
